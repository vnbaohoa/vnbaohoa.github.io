Project Introduction

The Cube Sweeper Robot was created for the Messy Room Competition, where robots must autonomously clear their side of the playing field by pushing foam cubes to the opponentâ€™s side within one minute. Our design uses wide servo-driven arms to sweep the entire board rather than detect individual cubes, improving reliability and minimizing sensing complexity.

System Overview

The robot integrates mechanical, electrical, and software subsystems:

  • Mechanical: 3D-printed bracket and arms controlled by micro-servos.
  • Electrical: Arduino Uno, TCS3200 color sensor, and QTI reflectance sensors.
  • Software: A custom finite-state machine coordinating navigation, sweeping, color sensing, and recovery modes.

Design & Development Process

I led the sensing and control development, integrating the QTI boundary tracking system, TCS3200 color detection, and servo motion control. Much of the development focused on tuning thresholds, calibrating 90Â° turns, and refining sensor behavior through repeated field testing.

Sensing & Control Strategy

The robot follows the right border using QTI reflectance sensors, detects midline color changes using the TCS3200 sensor, and performs time-calibrated rotations to sweep lane by lane. A state-machine structure ensures predictable transitions and modular control for each movement process.

Testing and Performance

The robot correctly detected field colors under most lighting conditions and performed stable sweeping cycles during practice. Competition testing revealed overshoot issues at higher speeds due to limited sensor sampling timeâ€”an important finding that informed potential future improvements.
